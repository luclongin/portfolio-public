Photobox website

Problem

All Photobox orders are made through their social media, making it time-consuming for both the owner and the customers.

Solution

An e-commerce website where users can view and buy products — and where the owner can view placed orders.

Context

Photobox sells printed framed pictures that stick to walls and leave no wall damage.

The current customer shopping experience ressembles this:

The owner reached out to me to design and build Photobox's e-commerce website.

Research

I made calls to understand why they thought they needed a website. I also interviewed 5 existing customers to identify any pain points. Here are some high-level findings:

  • ⌛ For the owner, the ordering process is too time-consuming.
  • 📈 It is difficult to convert leads into sales. Only 1 in 9 people who send a direct message end up buying something.
  • 👑 Most customers value the tailored shopping experience. Direct messages through social media for Q&A builds trust.
  • 🖼 The owner edits and crops customers' photos for the best results. Most customers would prefer to be shown a preview of the finished product before buying.

Photobox's main customer base is mothers with a desire to decorate their houses in a clean and practical way.


Photobox only has 1 direct competitor in the country, Wolpic, who sell their frames only through their website.

Ideation

Firstly, it was necessary to narrow down our scope and focus on deploying a first version of the website. The two main requirements were:

  • The customer can complete an order. Customer can select a product, upload photos, preview and crop photos, add delivery information and pay.

  • The owner can view all order summaries and uploaded photos.

Prototyping

I started testing a low-fi prototype on the owner to be sure to reveal any major blockers. Hi-fi prototypes were then tested on past customers and potential future customers.

The first two rounds of test and iteration fared quite well, with slight modifications each round. The main visual change was to the photo previewing page as seen below.

Tech stack

Before going forward, I researched and defined the required set of technologies for the project.

  • Front-end: JavaScript using the React framework, along with external libraries to upload and handle images.

  • Back-end: a basic CRUD system with Express and a MySQL database.

However, I realized that handling card payment using popular international frameworks would end up costing my client too much in fees.

I chose to incorporate the MercadoPago API for card payment and modified the prototype to account for the MercadoPago popup.
